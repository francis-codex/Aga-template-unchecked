]]> By: Anon https://sweeten.com/sweeten-renovations/family-renovates-brooklyn-brownstone/#comment-79831 Sat, 29 Jul 2017 03:52:57 +0000 https://blog-v2.sweeten.com/?p=25514#comment-79831 Here’s a suggestion for the window protection issue that I’ve used, though it may not be elegant enough for your bathroom. I installed a shower curtain rod across the wall, drilling into the tile using plastic anchors to secure it. Then I installed a clear vinyl shower curtain with a raised pattern in it – it let sunlight into the room, but the pattern was enough to obscure the view in from the outside (though it looks like you’ve done this already with the window panes used.) Having the rod across the whole wall meant I could move the curtain over when I wanted to open the window. You could cut the curtain it to your desired length at the bottom. I was putting the same shower curtain rod and shower curtain on the other side of the tub, so it looked good in my place – with your glass there, you may not want a vinyl shower curtain on a shower curtain rod in there. But it did protect all the wood trim and the wood window well. Perhaps you can find a more elegant sheer material that is water-resistant, and use a smaller curtain rod than a typical shower curtain rod.
