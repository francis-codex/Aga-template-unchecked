Santiago started his contracting career in a family business, then branched out on his own

Check out Santiago’s portfolio here

Where are you from and how long have you been in construction?

Originally, I’m from Spain. I have been in business for 15 years.

How did you get into construction?

My uncle used to own his own business, so I started working for him at 17 and have been doing that ever since. In 2001, I started up my own company.

Who is your ideal client?

I like working with clients who are open to creative solutions and input.

What do you love most about construction?

I like challenging myself.

If you weren’t doing construction, what would you be doing?

I would be maybe flipping houses, which is not the same thing as construction—almost, but not the same.

What is a challenge that homeowners have when starting a renovation project?

There are a lot of different pieces to consider. For example, in the kitchen, there are faucets, sinks, cabinets, flooring…it is very overwhelming, but we try to make it easy.

Subway or cab?

Both!

Favorite New York City restaurant?

El Porron, a Spanish restaurant.
